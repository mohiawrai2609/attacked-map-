France records ~1,000 excess deaths in five days as record-breaking European heatwave peaks; Europe-wide toll exceeds 1,300

France's national public health agency, Santé publique France, disclosed on 28 June 2026 approximately 1,000 excess deaths between 24-28 June, driven by a historic, climate-change-amplified heat dome that pushed a national-area high of 44.3C (Pissos, Landes, 23 June) and a Paris June record of 40.9C. Roughly 85% of the dead were aged 65+, and the agency warned the figure is a preliminary underestimate pending data from care homes and private residences (Paris alone recorded 109 deaths on 26 June versus a normal 7). The event is the second and most severe wave of the 2026 European heatwaves, peaking 28-29 June across Western and Central Europe. World Weather Attribution found the June heat was made ~3.5C hotter by fossil-fuel-driven climate change and would have been "virtually impossible" in 1976, calling it the most severe heatwave recorded over the studied region. Cascading impacts included EDF nuclear reactor shutdowns/curtailments (~4 GW, ~6% of French capacity) at Nogent-sur-Seine, Bugey and other sites as river temperatures breached environmental limits; record Belgian power prices (EUR 1,038/MWh); tens of thousands of power outages; transport disruption (cracked autobahn concrete, suspended Leipzig trams, a Swedish freight derailment); wildfire alerts across France and Iberia; and dozens of drowning deaths. Europe-wide excess deaths exceeded 1,300 by 28 June (Spain 327, UK ~25). This is a mass-casualty climate/natural-hazard environmental incident with material public-health, energy-infrastructure and economic consequences.

Strait of Hormuz ceasefire falters as US-Iran trade strikes, threatening renewed shutdown of the world's most critical oil chokepoint

On 28 June 2026, Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) launched ballistic missiles and drones at the US Ali Al Salem airbase in Kuwait and the US Fifth Fleet at Port Salman in Bahrain, retaliating for US airstrikes a day earlier on five Iranian targets (Sirik, Bandar-e Lengeh, Qeshm Island). The US strikes themselves answered a drone attack on the Panama-flagged crude tanker Kiku (carrying 2M+ barrels) near the strait. The exchange threatened to reverse a fragile, partial recovery in Strait of Hormuz transit that had only just resumed under an IRGC daily-quota / toll system following the 28 February 2026 closure, which cut chokepoint flows by ~90-95% (crude), ~99% (LNG) and ~94% (fertilizer cargoes). Iran threatened a "complete halt" to ceasefire talks under the 17 June US-Iran MOU (Trump-Pezeshkian), declaring the strait "under the total oversight and management of Iran" for the coming 30 days and designating mandatory shipping lanes (north of Larak Island), with tolls reported at $1-2M per vessel. Major container lines Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM and Hapag-Lloyd had suspended transits during the closure (war-risk insurance was withdrawn 5 March), forcing Cape of Good Hope reroutes; ~22,500 mariners on 1,550+ vessels remained trapped in/around the strait. Transits stood at ~5 ships/day versus a normal ~93/day. One Qatari citizen was killed by shrapnel; Bahrain reported a damaged residential building near its airport. Brent, which had fallen to ~$72/bbl on hopes of normalisation (down from a March peak near $118-126/bbl, +64%), faces renewed upside risk; EIA's closed-strait scenario models Brent averaging $105/bbl. The chokepoint carries ~13M bpd of crude (about one-fifth of global oil consumption) and ~20% of global LNG.

Hijacked npm and Go packages abuse hidden VS Code autorun tasks and blockchain dead-drops to deploy a cross-platform Python infostealer targeting developers

npm registry (html-to-gutenberg, fetch-page-assets) and Go module ecosystem (16 packages incl. lambda-platform/lambda, reauheau/goaubio, glacialspring/go-winsparkle) — "Fake Font" / Contagious Interview campaign (DPRK-linked) · Global (open-source software supply chain; campaign attributed to North Korean threat actors) · Software & Cloud Infrastructure

JFrog Security Research disclosed on June 29, 2026 that two hijacked npm packages — html-to-gutenberg v4.2.11 (XRAY-1008590) and fetch-page-assets v1.2.9 (XRAY-1008535), both uploaded May 25, 2026 — were weaponized with a novel infection chain that bypasses npm lifecycle scripts. Instead, the malware hides its payload in a fake font asset (public/fonts/fa-solid-400.woff2, padded with 752 space characters to look empty) and executes it via a hidden VS Code/Cursor task named "eslint-check" configured with runOn:"folderOpen" — meaning the malware fires automatically the moment a developer opens the trusted project folder in their editor, with no install or build step required. The loader uses a blockchain dead-drop scheme: it resolves an encrypted JavaScript payload from transaction input data on TronGrid (api.trongrid.io), falling back to Aptos (fullnode.mainnet.aptoslabs.com) and BSC JSON-RPC (bsc-dataseed.binance.org, bsc-rpc.publicnode.com), extracting bytes after a "?.?" marker and XOR-decoding them. The multi-stage chain (victim marker _V, /$/boot beacon with Sec-V header, socket.io backdoor with ss_eval/ss_eval64 remote code execution, a runtime bootstrapper that drops ~/.node_modules and a portable Python runtime) culminates in a cross-platform Python infostealer harvesting Chromium/Firefox credentials, password managers (1Password, LastPass, Bitwarden), crypto wallets (MetaMask, Phantom, TronLink, Trust Wallet, Coinbase), Git/GitHub Desktop and VS Code data, and OS credential stores (Windows Credential Manager, macOS Keychain, Linux Secret Service). On June 25, 2026, Nextron Systems identified 16 additional Go modules carrying the identical malware and fake-font structure — most are legitimate packages (e.g., github.com/lambda-platform/lambda, reauheau/goaubio, glacialspring/go-winsparkle) whose latest released version was trojanized alongside original contents. The campaign is tracked as "Fake Font" by Paul McCarty (OpenSourceMalware) and assessed as a DPRK-linked Contagious Interview variant, reflecting continuity with North Korean financially-motivated developer-targeting operations (BeaverTail/InvisibleFerret lineage). The npm packages have been removed; Go modules and blockchain dead-drops remained a live concern at disclosure.

On the evening of Saturday 27 June 2026, Qatar's General Directorate of Coasts and Borders Security (Ministry of Interior) noticed during routine maritime monitoring that a small vessel carrying two people had failed to return at its scheduled time, and launched a search-and-rescue operation. The death underscores how spillover munition debris from the US-Iran exchange is now generating civilian casualties across Gulf states even outside the direct target set (Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan).

HIGHCyber27 Jun

SSU and FBI expose Russian FSB-linked campaign hijacking Signal/WhatsApp accounts of officials across Ukraine, Europe and the US

On 27 June 2026, the Security Service of Ukraine (SSU), acting jointly with the U.S. FBI, publicly exposed a long-running Russian intelligence services (RIS) campaign to compromise commercial messaging-application (CMA) accounts — principally Signal and WhatsApp — belonging to government officials, military personnel, politicians, journalists and activists across Ukraine, Europe and the United States. The disclosure landed one day after the FBI/CISA reissued IC3 PSA I-062626 (26 June 2026), an update to the original March 2026 advisory (PSA260320). Operators impersonate a CMA "support bot" via SMS, typically sent in early-morning hours when targets are least attentive, to harvest account credentials and verification codes. The campaign has evolved beyond the QR-code "linked devices" abuse documented by Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) in Feb 2025 (groups UNC5792/UAC-0195 and UNC4221/UAC-0185, plus Star Blizzard): attackers now coax victims into enabling chat backups and surrendering their Signal Backup Recovery Key. That key remains valid even after a victim creates a new account on the same phone number, granting durable access to historical, private and group messages, contact lists, and a launchpad for onward phishing. FBI characterizes the activity as conducted by the Russian Federal Security Service (FSB), FSB Border Guards and Russian military services. This is a state-sponsored espionage operation against high-intelligence-value individuals; no single confirmed account-count was published, though related April 2026 activity breached 170+ Ukrainian prosecutors' email accounts and the FBI describes account compromises numbering in the thousands across prior CMA campaigns.

MEDIUMEnvironmental28 Jun

Wildfire in WWII-ordnance-contaminated German nature reserve forces full evacuation of Traisen

Traisen / Bad Kreuznach district, Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany · Germany

On 27 June 2026, at the peak of Europe's record-breaking June heat dome, a forest fire ignited around 14:30 local time in the Rotenfels nature reserve near Traisen in the Bad Kreuznach district of Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany. The woodland is a former WWII munitions disposal/military site contaminated with unexploded ordnance (UXO). As the fire spread, buried WWII munitions detonated repeatedly, forcing firefighters to suspend direct ground attack; an ordnance disposal (Kampfmittelräumdienst) unit was deployed to continuously assess the area. The fire grew from roughly 3,000 m2 to an estimated 10,000+ m2 (Copernicus EMS activation EMSR886). Authorities first ordered a 1-km partial evacuation (residents out by ~02:00) and then, on the afternoon of 28 June, ordered the complete evacuation of Traisen — approximately 650 residents. Up to ~250 emergency personnel from multiple counties were deployed, supported by firefighting helicopters drawing water from the Niederhausen reservoir. The Rotenfels is the highest cliff massif north of the Alps (~327 m), a designated nature reserve hosting rare flora/fauna (peregrine falcons, eagle owls). The incident is one of several heat-driven wildfires in German UXO-contaminated forests in the same window (notably Gohrischheide in Saxony), occurring as Germany set a provisional national heat record of 41.7°C and Europe recorded 1,300+ excess deaths since 21 June. No fatalities reported in Traisen; this is a climate-physical natural-hazard incident compounded by legacy UXO contamination.

Canadian offshore regulator discloses Hibernia oil-platform spill posed 'deadly' explosion risk; investigates operator

Hibernia Management and Development Company (HMDC) · Canada

On June 28, 2026, the Canada-Newfoundland and Labrador Offshore Energy Regulator (C-NLOPB) publicly disclosed that a May 12, 2026 incident aboard the ExxonMobil-operated Hibernia gravity-base platform (~315 km east of St. John's, Newfoundland) was a "major hydrocarbon release" that the regulator classified as having potential for fire, explosion and fatality. While crews were preparing to offload crude to a shuttle tanker around 11 a.m., they heard unusual noises from the platform's utility shaft; a drain on a sludge pump had sheared off, releasing roughly 1,600 litres of crude oil, and gas was also detected. A worker stopped the discharge by closing a manual valve on the broken pump. No one was injured and none of the crude reached the ocean (it was contained on the platform). Operator HMDC submitted preliminary investigation findings on May 27, 2026; the C-NLOPB has requested further information and is investigating root causes and the company's response. HMDC carries elevated enforcement exposure: it pleaded guilty and paid CAD 400,000 in 2023 over a 2019 ocean spill (~12,000 litres), was penalized CAD 28,000 for an August 2019 release, and faced earlier charges related to 2015 loading-system spills. This is a regulatory/safety enforcement event (potential process-safety failure and open investigation) rather than an environmental discharge to sea, because the release was contained on-platform.

Five search-and-rescue teams operated in Madison County and over 600 people were rescued statewide by boat and aircraft as the flooding moved across the commonwealth. All 120 Kentucky counties were under flood warnings simultaneously, a near-unprecedented footprint, with more rain forecast to push east on Sunday June 28. Beshear warned the death toll could rise. This is the third major Kentucky flood disaster in under four years (July 2022, February 2025), underscoring an accelerating extreme-precipitation pattern in central and eastern Kentucky.

MEDIUMCyber27 Jun

Polymarket loss from supply-chain frontend attack revised up to $3.1M as platform pledges full refunds

Polymarket · United States

On June 25, 2026, attackers compromised a third-party frontend vendor whose JavaScript is loaded by Polymarket's website, injecting malicious code that tricked a subset of users into approving fraudulent transactions on the legitimate polymarket.com domain. The script drained pUSD (Polymarket's USDC-backed stablecoin, also referred to as ParionUSD) from roughly 11-15 user wallets. The attacker swapped the proceeds and bridged them from Polygon to Ethereum, consolidating into approximately 1,893 ETH in attacker-controlled addresses. Polymarket's core smart contracts were NOT breached — this was a classic software supply-chain / client-side dependency compromise of the web delivery layer, a class of risk that smart-contract audits do not cover. Polymarket removed the malicious dependency, said it contained the incident, and pledged to refund all affected users in full. On June 27, blockchain-intelligence firm AMLBot revised the loss figure upward to $3.1M (from initial estimates of ~$2.9M), and the platform reiterated its full-refund commitment. The incident is Polymarket's third security event of 2026 (following a March/May internal-wallet/private-key drain of ~$520K-$700K on Polygon and a December 2025 third-party login-provider incident) and lands while the platform is simultaneously under a CFTC investigation into deceptive influencer-marketing practices — compounding reputational and regulatory pressure on the prediction-market operator.
